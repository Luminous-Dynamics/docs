# Community Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open, welcoming, and consciousness-first environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to a compassionate, dignified, and healthy community where technology serves human flourishing.

## Consciousness-First Principles

Our community is guided by consciousness-first computing principles:

### 1. Respect Attention
- **Be concise**: Value others' time and attention
- **Stay focused**: Keep discussions on-topic and productive
- **Reduce noise**: Quality over quantity in communication

### 2. Amplify Capability
- **Empower others**: Share knowledge generously
- **Support growth**: Welcome questions from all skill levels
- **Enable independence**: Teach people to solve their own problems

### 3. Maintain Transparency
- **Communicate openly**: Share reasoning and decision-making process
- **Acknowledge limitations**: It's okay to say "I don't know"
- **Surface conflicts**: Address issues directly and respectfully

### 4. Enable Sovereignty
- **Respect autonomy**: Everyone makes their own choices
- **Avoid manipulation**: No guilt-tripping, pressure, or coercion
- **Honor boundaries**: Accept "no" gracefully

### 5. Serve Wellbeing
- **Prioritize humans**: Technology serves people, not the reverse
- **Foster belonging**: Create spaces where everyone can participate
- **Support wholeness**: Recognize people as complete beings, not just their code contributions

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- **Using welcoming and inclusive language**
- **Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences**
- **Gracefully accepting constructive criticism**
- **Focusing on what is best for the community**
- **Showing empathy towards other community members**
- **Giving credit generously** and acknowledging others' contributions
- **Assuming good intent** while remaining open to impact
- **Asking questions** rather than making assumptions
- **Offering help proactively** to those who are struggling
- **Celebrating others' successes** without jealousy

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **Harassment and discrimination** of any kind
- **The use of sexualized language or imagery** and unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- **Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments**, and personal or political attacks
- **Public or private harassment**
- **Publishing others' private information** without explicit permission
- **Spam or self-promotion** unrelated to the community
- **Attention manipulation**: Clickbait, manufactured urgency, guilt-tripping
- **Gatekeeping**: "You're not a real [X]" or "Everyone knows [Y]"
- **Condescension**: Making others feel small or stupid for not knowing something
- **Dismissing concerns**: "It's not a big deal" or "You're too sensitive"
- **Other conduct** which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

### Special Guidance

#### On Technical Disagreements

Technical disagreements are normal and healthy:

- **Focus on ideas, not people**: "This approach might not scale" vs. "You don't understand scaling"
- **Provide reasoning**: Explain *why* you disagree
- **Stay humble**: You might be wrong; they might have context you lack
- **Seek understanding**: "Help me understand your thinking" is more productive than "That's wrong"
- **Agree to disagree**: Not all disagreements need resolution

#### On Helping Beginners

Everyone was a beginner once:

- **Never say "just"**: "Just do X" makes it sound trivial when it's not
- **Avoid RTFM**: If docs answered their question well, they wouldn't ask
- **Provide context**: Don't just give answers, explain the "why"
- **Celebrate questions**: "Great question!" helps more than you think
- **Update docs**: If many people ask the same thing, the docs need improvement

#### On Giving Feedback

Feedback helps us all grow:

- **Be specific**: "The documentation is confusing" â†’ "Section X could use more examples"
- **Be kind**: You can be honest and compassionate simultaneously
- **Suggest alternatives**: Don't just criticize, offer better approaches
- **Review intentions**: Feedback should help, not punish or shame
- **Accept rejection**: Your suggestion might not be implemented, and that's okay

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all community spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories (issues, PRs, discussions)
- Discord server
- Community forums
- Email communications
- Events (virtual or in-person)
- Social media when representing the community

It also applies when representing the community in public spaces. Examples include using an official email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to:

**Email**: conduct@luminousdynamics.org

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter.

**What to include in a report:**

- Your contact information
- Names (real, usernames, or nicknames) of any individuals involved
- Your account of what occurred, including any relevant context
- Links to public records (GitHub comments, Discord messages, etc.)
- Any other information you believe would be helpful

### Confidentiality

All reports will be kept confidential to the extent possible. We will only share information:

- With community moderators involved in investigating the report
- When legally required
- With your explicit permission

Reporters will not be retaliated against for good-faith reports.

### Investigation Process

1. **Acknowledgment** within 24 hours of receiving the report
2. **Investigation**: Review evidence, interview involved parties if needed
3. **Decision**: Determine if Code of Conduct was violated
4. **Action**: Implement appropriate consequences (see below)
5. **Follow-up**: Check in with reporter about resolution

### Consequences

Community moderators may take any action they deem appropriate, including:

**Warning**: Private, written warning with clarity about the violation and expected behavior changes.

**Temporary Ban**: Temporary ban from community spaces (GitHub, Discord, etc.). Duration specified based on severity.

**Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from all community spaces. Reserved for severe or repeated violations.

**Public Statement**: In cases affecting community safety, a public statement may be made (without identifying the reporter).

### Appeals

If you believe a moderation decision was made in error, you can appeal to:

**Email**: conduct-appeals@luminousdynamics.org

Appeals will be reviewed by community leaders not involved in the original decision.

## Community Moderators

Current community moderators:

- Community moderator team (conduct@luminousdynamics.org)

Moderators are expected to model the Code of Conduct and are held to higher standards. Reports about moderators will be handled by other moderators or project leadership.
