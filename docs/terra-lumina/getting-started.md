# Getting Started with Terra Lumina

Welcome to Terra Lumina! This guide will walk you through your first investment in renewable energy and community ownership.

## What You'll Learn

By the end of this guide, you'll know how to:

- Create and verify your account
- Browse and evaluate projects
- Make your first investment
- Track your portfolio
- Understand returns and distributions

**Time required:** 30-45 minutes

## Prerequisites

Before you begin:

- **Age:** 18+ years old
- **Location:** Currently accepting investors from US (accredited), EU, UK, and Switzerland
- **Funds:** Minimum $10 to invest (we recommend starting with $50-100 to learn)
- **Documents:** Government-issued ID for verification
- **Bank account:** For receiving distributions

!!! info "Accredited Investor Requirement (US Only)"
    Due to SEC regulations, US investors must currently be accredited (annual income >$200K or net worth >$1M excluding primary residence). We're working to expand access to all US investors via Regulation Crowdfunding.

Required by law for anti-money laundering compliance.

1. **Upload** government-issued ID:

   - Driver's license + proof of address

2. **Take** a selfie (for identity confirmation)

3. **Wait** for verification (usually 15 minutes to 24 hours)

**You'll receive email confirmation when verified.**

### Link Bank Account

For receiving investment distributions:

1. **Go to** Profile â†’ Payment Methods
2. **Click** "Add Bank Account"
3. **Enter** bank details:
   - Account number
   - Routing number (US) / Sort code (UK) / IBAN (EU)
   - Account holder name

4. **Verify** via micro-deposits:
   - We'll send two small deposits (< $1 each)
   - Check your bank in 1-2 business days
   - Enter the amounts to confirm

**For security, only verified banks can receive distributions.**

## Step 3: Make Your First Investment

### Choose a Project

**Criteria to consider:**

- **Risk level:** Start with lower risk (4-5 stars)
- **Location:** Support your local area or anywhere
- **Impact:** What resonates with your values?
- **Returns:** Match your financial goals
- **Timeline:** When do you need liquidity?

**Recommendation:** For your first investment, choose a 4-5 star project in a renewable energy type you understand (solar, wind, or hydro).

### Investment Amount

**How much to invest:**

- **Minimum:** $10 (legal minimum)
- **Recommended first investment:** $50-100
- **Diversification:** Plan to invest in 5-10 projects over time
- **Risk management:** Only invest what you can afford to lose

**Example portfolio:**

- $100 each in 5 different projects = $500 total
- Mix of energy types, locations, and risk levels
- Provides diversification and learning

## Understanding Returns

### What to Expect

**Target IRR:** 8-14% annually

**Reality:** Actual returns vary by project

**Example timeline for $100 investment at 11% IRR:**

| Year | Distribution | Cumulative | Project Status |
|------|--------------|------------|----------------|
| 1 | $11 | $11 | Construction/Ramp up |
| 2 | $11 | $22 | Full operations |
| 3 | $11 | $33 | Stable operations |
| 4 | $11 | $44 | Stable operations |
| 5 | $11 | $55 | Preparing transition |
| 6 | $11 | $66 | Transition begins |
| 7 | $11 | $77 | Community ownership |

**By year 7:** You've received $77 on $100 investment (77% return), project transfers to community.

**After year 7:** Project continues serving community, owned locally.

### Risks & Realities

**What could reduce returns:**

- Less energy production than projected (weather, equipment)
- Higher operating costs than expected
- Regulatory changes affecting revenue
- Equipment failures requiring repairs
- Grid connection delays

**What could increase returns:**

- Better than expected production
- Lower operating costs
- Electricity price increases
- Additional revenue streams (carbon credits, grid services)

**Important:** This is real investing with real risks. Some projects may underperform or fail. Diversification helps manage this risk.

## Regenerative Exit Explained

### The Journey

Terra Lumina's unique model:

**Years 0-3: Traditional Investment**
- You own a share of the project
- Receive quarterly distributions
- Professional management
- Standard investor rights

**Years 3-5: Community Participation**
- Local community gets board seats
- Shared decision-making begins
- Community training and engagement
- Transparent transition planning

**Years 5-7: Transition Preparation**
- Community organization forms (trust, co-op, or municipal)
- Skills transfer and training
- Investor obligations being fulfilled
- Final distributions calculated

**Year 7+: Community Ownership**
- Full ownership transfers to community
- You've received your returns
- Project serves community forever
- You can reinvest in new projects

### Why This Matters

**For you:**
- Competitive financial returns
- Lasting impact beyond your investment
- Values-aligned investing
- Potential tax benefits for transition

**For communities:**
- Local ownership of critical infrastructure
- Revenue stays in community
- Jobs and economic development
- Energy independence
