# Frequently Asked Questions

## Investment Basics

### What is Terra Lumina?

Terra Lumina is a renewable energy investment platform that democratizes access to clean energy projects. We enable anyone to invest as little as $10 in local renewable energy infrastructure while creating a path to community ownership through our Regenerative Exit model.

### What is the minimum investment?

**$10** - We believe everyone should have access to renewable energy investment, regardless of wealth. You can invest any amount from $10 to millions per project.

### Is this really investing, or is it a donation?

**This is real investing** with expected financial returns. It's not charity. You own a share of energy projects that generate revenue, and you receive returns based on performance. However, unlike traditional investments, our Regenerative Exit model transitions ownership to communities over time.

### What returns can I expect?

**Target range**: 8-14% IRR (Internal Rate of Return) over 5-7 years.

!!! warning "Important Disclaimers"
    - Returns are **not guaranteed** and depend on project performance
    - Past performance does not indicate future results
    - Energy markets and regulations can change
    - Individual project risks vary significantly
    - You could lose some or all of your investment

Always invest only what you can afford to lose. This is a long-term investment, not a savings account.

### How long until I get my money back?

**Typical timeline:**

- **Quarterly distributions**: Start 3-6 months after project completion
- **Full return of capital**: 5-7 years on average
- **Total investment period**: 7-10 years including community transition

This is a **long-term investment**. If you need liquidity sooner, Terra Lumina may not be suitable for you.

### Can I sell my investment before maturity?

Currently, there is **limited liquidity**. We're working on:

- Secondary marketplace (planned 2026)
- Buyback programs for hardship cases
- Transfer to other investors (with approval)

For now, plan to hold your investment for the full term.

## Getting Started

### Who can invest?

**Currently accepting investors from:**

- United States (accredited investors only, per SEC regulations)
- European Union (all investors above age 18)
- United Kingdom (all investors above age 18)
- Switzerland (all investors above age 18)

**Accredited investor definition (US):**

- Annual income >$200K (individual) or >$300K (joint) for past 2 years, OR
- Net worth >$1M (excluding primary residence)

We're actively working to expand access globally and to non-accredited US investors through Regulation Crowdfunding.

### How do I get started?

1. **Create account**: [atlas.luminousdynamics.io](https://atlas.luminousdynamics.io)
2. **Complete KYC**: Identity verification (required by law)
3. **Browse projects**: Explore available investments
4. **Invest**: Choose amount and confirm
5. **Track**: Monitor performance in your dashboard

Typically takes 15-30 minutes for your first investment.

### What information do I need to provide?

**Required for all investors:**

- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Residential address
- Government-issued ID
- Tax identification number
- Bank account details (for distributions)

**Additional for US accredited investors:**

- Income verification OR
- Net worth documentation

We take privacy seriously and never sell your data.

### Are there any fees?

**Investment fees:**

- **2% platform fee** on each investment (one-time)
- **1% annual management fee** on project revenues
- **No exit fees** or redemption charges
- **No custody fees** for holding investments

**Example**: $100 investment = $2 platform fee, $98 goes to project. If project generates $10/year revenue, we keep $0.10, you get $9.90.

## Projects & Due Diligence

### What types of projects are available?

Current project types:

- **Hydroelectric**: Dam retrofits and run-of-river (87,000 US sites available)
- **Solar**: Community solar farms and rooftop installations
- **Wind**: Small-scale wind farms in high-wind areas
- **Energy Storage**: Battery systems for grid stability
- **Small Modular Reactors**: (Coming soon) Advanced nuclear microreactors

### How are projects vetted?

Every project goes through rigorous due diligence:

1. **Technical assessment**: Engineering feasibility, resource quality
2. **Financial modeling**: Revenue projections, cost estimates, risk analysis
3. **Legal review**: Permits, land rights, compliance
4. **Environmental impact**: Sustainability, community benefits
5. **Community engagement**: Local support, stakeholder alignment

Only ~10% of projects we review make it to the platform.

### Who manages the projects?

Projects are managed by:

- **Local developers**: Licensed, experienced project managers
- **Terra Lumina oversight**: Monitoring and reporting
- **Community boards**: Increasing involvement over time
- **Third-party auditors**: Independent verification

You can see full team credentials for each project.

### What risks are involved?

**Project-specific risks:**

- Construction delays or cost overruns
- Equipment failures or maintenance issues
- Weather/resource variability (less sun, water, wind than expected)
- Regulatory changes affecting revenue
- Grid connection issues

**Platform risks:**

- Terra Lumina business failure
- Changes in energy markets
- Currency fluctuations (for international projects)
- Cybersecurity incidents

**Mitigation:**

- Diversify across multiple projects
- Review risk ratings (all projects rated 1-5 stars)
- Start small and learn
- Read all project documentation

### Can I visit the projects I invest in?

**Yes!** We encourage investor engagement:

- Project site tours (coordinated through local teams)
- Community events and celebrations
- Virtual tours and live camera feeds
- Annual investor meetings

Many investors find this the most rewarding part of the experience.

### How are returns calculated?

Returns come from:

- **Energy sales**: Revenue from selling electricity to the grid
- **Renewable energy credits**: Environmental attribute certificates
- **Carbon credits**: Offset market value
- **Capacity payments**: Grid reliability services

Your share is proportional to your investment amount.

### When do I receive distributions?

**Quarterly distributions** after project begins operation:

- Q1: End of March
- Q2: End of June
- Q3: End of September
- Q4: End of December

Distributions are automatic to your linked bank account.

**This is complex and varies by jurisdiction:**

**EU investors:**

## Regenerative Exit Model

### What does "Regenerative Exit" mean?

Unlike traditional startups that "exit" by selling to the highest bidder (extracting value from the community), we "exit" by **transferring ownership to the communities** our projects serve.

**Timeline:**

- **Years 0-3**: Traditional investment structure, you receive returns
- **Years 3-5**: Community participation increases, shared governance
- **Years 5-7**: Gradual ownership transfer begins
- **Year 7+**: Full community ownership, you've received your returns

### Will I lose my investment when it transitions to community ownership?

**No!** By the time ownership transitions:

1. You've already received your principal back plus returns
2. The transition happens only after investor obligations are met
3. You may receive community appreciation bonuses
4. You'll have the option to reinvest in new projects

### Why would I want this?

Several reasons:

1. **Financial**: Competitive returns over the investment period
2. **Impact**: Lasting community benefit beyond your investment
3. **Legacy**: Projects continue serving communities for decades
4. **Tax benefits**: Potential charitable deduction for transition value
5. **Values alignment**: Invest in line with your principles

### How does community ownership work?

Projects transition to:

- **Community trusts**: Locally governed, democratically managed
- **Energy cooperatives**: Member-owned and operated
- **Municipal utilities**: City or county ownership

Communities receive training and support for successful transition.
