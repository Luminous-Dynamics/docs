## Migration Strategies

### Strategy 1: Gradual Allocation (Conservative)

**Best for:** Risk-averse investors, first-time impact investors

**Approach:**
```
Year 1: Allocate 2-5% of portfolio to Terra Lumina
        Start small ($100-500), learn the platform
        Monitor performance for 12 months

Year 2: Increase to 5-10% if comfortable
        Diversify across 3-5 projects
        Understand distribution cycles

Year 3+: Reach target allocation (10-20% for impact-focused)
         Mature understanding of risk/return
         Established project preferences
```

**Timeline:** 2-3 years to target allocation

**Example - $100K portfolio:**
- Year 1: $2,000 in Terra Lumina (2%)
- Year 2: $7,000 total (5% - add $5K)
- Year 3: $15,000 total (15% - add $8K)

**Pros:**
- Low risk of over-allocation
- Time to learn and adjust
- Can maintain liquidity elsewhere

**Cons:**
- Slower impact building
- Takes time to see full benefits

### Strategy 2: Direct Replacement (Moderate)

**Best for:** Experienced investors, those with clear allocation targets

**Approach:**
1. **Identify replacement portion** of portfolio
   - Example: Replace 10% bond allocation with Terra Lumina
2. **Sell equivalent traditional holdings**
   - Sell bonds over 6-12 months
3. **Invest proceeds in Terra Lumina**
   - Diversify across 5-10 projects
4. **Maintain allocation balance**
   - Rebalance annually

**Timeline:** 6-12 months

**Example - $250K portfolio with 30% bonds:**
- Current: $75K in bonds
- Goal: $37.5K in bonds, $37.5K in Terra Lumina (split)
- Action: Sell $37.5K bonds, invest in Terra Lumina over 6 months

**Pros:**
- Maintains overall portfolio risk profile
- Clear allocation framework
- Faster impact deployment

**Cons:**
- Taxable event (selling existing holdings)
- Reduced liquidity in that allocation
- Requires selling during migration

### Strategy 3: New Capital Allocation (Aggressive)

**Best for:** Active earners, those with new capital to deploy

**Approach:**
1. **Redirect new investment capital** to Terra Lumina
2. **Don't sell existing holdings** (avoid taxes)
3. **Build allocation over time** with fresh capital
4. **Eventually rebalance** if needed

**Timeline:** Ongoing

**Example - Annual $20K investment capacity:**
- Year 1: 100% of new capital â†’ Terra Lumina ($20K)
- Year 2: 50% Terra Lumina, 50% traditional ($10K each)
- Year 3+: Target split (e.g., 20% Terra Lumina, 80% traditional)

**Pros:**
- No need to sell existing holdings
- Tax-efficient
- Flexible pacing

**Cons:**
- Slower to target allocation
- Requires ongoing new capital
- May create imbalanced portfolio short-term

### Strategy 4: Dedicated Impact Allocation (Focused)

**Best for:** Committed impact investors, those with separate impact goals

**Approach:**
1. **Create separate "impact bucket"** in portfolio
2. **Fund with 5-25% of total portfolio**
3. **Allocate 100% of impact bucket** to Terra Lumina
4. **Manage separately** from traditional holdings

**Timeline:** 1-2 months to establish

**Example - $500K portfolio, 20% impact allocation:**
- Total portfolio: $500K
- Impact allocation: $100K (20%)
- Terra Lumina allocation: $100K (all of impact bucket)
- Traditional allocation: $400K (remaining 80%)

**Pros:**
- Clear impact/traditional separation
- Easy to track impact performance
- Doesn't complicate traditional allocation

**Cons:**
- Requires sufficient capital for diversity
- May concentrate risk in impact bucket
- Separate mental accounting needed

## Account and Tax Considerations

### Account Types

**Where you CAN invest in Terra Lumina:**
- âœ… Individual taxable accounts
- âœ… Joint accounts
- âœ… Trust accounts
- âœ… Self-directed IRAs (special setup required)
- âœ… Business/entity accounts

**Where you currently CANNOT:**
- âŒ Standard IRA/401(k) (coming Q3 2026)
- âŒ 529 education savings
- âŒ HSA accounts

**Coming in 2026:**
- Self-directed IRA integration
- Potential 401(k) access (depends on provider)

### Tax Implications

**Distribution income:**
- Taxed as ordinary income (like REIT distributions)
- Reported on Form 1099-DIV
- Subject to federal and state income tax
- NOT qualified dividends (different tax treatment)

**Return of capital:**
- May be partially tax-deferred
- Reduces cost basis
- Taxed at exit if cost basis reaches zero
- Reported on 1099-DIV

**Exit/redemption:**
- Potential capital gain/loss depending on structure
- Long-term capital gains treatment (held > 1 year)
- Transfer to community is not taxable to investor

**Compared to traditional investments:**

| Investment | Tax Treatment | Terra Lumina |
|------------|---------------|--------------|
| Stock dividends | Qualified (0-20% rate) | Ordinary income (10-37%) |
| Bond interest | Ordinary income | Ordinary income |
| REIT distributions | Ordinary income | Ordinary income (similar) |
| Stock sale gains | Cap gains (0-20%) | Cap gains (0-20%) |

**Tax efficiency tips:**
- Hold in taxable accounts if in low tax bracket
- Consider self-directed Roth IRA (tax-free growth)
- Time distributions with lower-income years if possible
- Consult tax advisor for your situation

### Transferring Existing Accounts

**Can I transfer stocks/bonds into Terra Lumina?**
No. You must:
1. Sell existing holdings
2. Transfer cash
3. Invest in Terra Lumina projects

**This creates taxable events** (selling existing holdings).

**Tax-efficient transfer strategy:**
1. Sell in tranches over 12-24 months
2. Harvest losses to offset gains
3. Time sales during lower-income years
4. Prioritize highest-cost-basis holdings first (lower tax)

**Alternative:** Use new capital instead of selling (Strategy 3 above).

### Understanding Different Risk Types

**Stock market risks you know:**
- Market volatility
- Company performance
- Economic cycles
- Sector risks
- Geopolitical events

**Terra Lumina risks (different):**
- Project completion risk (construction delays)
- Technology/equipment risk (performance below expected)
- Regulatory risk (policy changes)
- Counterparty risk (off-taker credit)
- Natural disaster risk (weather events)

**Key difference:**
- Stocks: Correlated to market sentiment and macroeconomics
- Terra Lumina: Tied to specific project execution and energy demand

**This means:**
- âœ… Diversification benefit (uncorrelated returns)

### Using the Five Pillars for Due Diligence

Instead of reading 10-Ks and analyst reports, you evaluate:

**1. Financial Pillar:**
- Revenue projections realistic?
- Third-party validation of returns?
- Conservative assumptions?

**2. Technical Pillar:**
- Proven technology (not experimental)?
- Reputable equipment suppliers?
- Technical due diligence completed?

**3. Community Pillar:**
- Local support for project?
- Job creation plans?
- Community transition plan?

**4. Risk Pillar:**
- Identified and disclosed risks?
- Mitigation strategies in place?
- Insurance coverage adequate?

**5. Values Pillar:**
- Transparent reporting?
- Community ownership commitment?
- Environmental impact positive?

## Common Migration Questions

### "How do I diversify with only $500?"

**Strategy:**
- Start with $100-200 per project
- Invest in 3-5 different projects
- Different technologies (solar, wind)
- Different geographies (US, international)
- Different stages (operating, construction)

**Example $500 allocation:**
- $150 in operating US solar (low risk)
- $150 in construction EU wind (medium risk)
- $100 in planning Peru hydro (higher risk, emerging market)
- $100 in storage project (technology diversity)

**Better:** Save to $1,000+ for more meaningful diversity.

### "What if I need the money before 10 years?"

**Options:**
1. **Hardship redemption** (serious financial need)
   - Medical emergencies
   - Job loss
   - Unexpected expenses
   - Approved on case-by-case basis
   - Redeemed at fair value

2. **Transfer to family/friends**
   - Can transfer ownership
   - Buyer takes on remaining term
   - Subject to KYC/AML
   - No markup allowed

3. **Secondary market** (coming Q2 2026)
   - Pilot program for early exit
   - May trade at discount to fair value
   - Limited liquidity

**Planning tip:** Only invest capital you can commit for 10 years.

### "How do I monitor performance compared to my stock portfolio?"

**Tracking methods:**

1. **Terra Lumina dashboard**
   - Login to see all projects

   - Distribution history

3. **Portfolio trackers**

**Expectation setting:**
