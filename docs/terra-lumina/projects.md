**Project-specific Q&A:**

**Q: What happens if solar production is lower than expected?**

A: The project has performance insurance that guarantees 90% of projected energy production. If actual production falls below this, insurance covers the shortfall. Additionally, our projections use conservative assumptions (P90 confidence level), meaning there's a 90% probability that actual production will *exceed* projections.

**Q: What if the utility doesn't pay for the electricity?**

A: The Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) is with Nevada Energy, an A-rated utility backed by Berkshire Hathaway Energy. Additionally, the PPA includes payment security provisions and is backed by a letter of credit. Risk of non-payment is extremely low.

**Q: How will I receive returns?**

A: Quarterly distributions are deposited directly to your Terra Lumina account. You can withdraw to your bank account, reinvest in other projects, or accumulate for the exit.

## Project Categories

### By Technology

#### Solar Projects

**Characteristics:**
- Predictable generation (solar data widely available)
- Low operating costs
- Proven technology
- Scalable from small to utility-scale

**Best for:**
- Conservative investors
- First-time renewable energy investors
- Sunny regions (Southwest US, Spain, Australia, India)

**Example projects:**
- Community solar (1-5 MW, $100k-$500k rounds)
- Rooftop solar arrays (100-500 kW, $50k-$200k rounds)
- Utility-scale farms (50-200 MW, $2M-$10M rounds)

#### Wind Projects

**Characteristics:**
- Higher capacity factors than solar
- Location-dependent (coastal, plains, highlands)
- Larger scale (typically 50+ MW)
- More complex technology

**Best for:**
- Moderate risk tolerance
- Longer investment timelines (10-15 years)
- Diversification from solar

**Example projects:**
- Onshore wind farms (50-200 MW, $5M-$20M rounds)
- Offshore wind (500+ MW, $20M+ rounds, institutional)
- Community wind (5-20 MW, $500k-$2M rounds)

#### Hydro Projects

**Characteristics:**
- Most reliable (24/7 generation possible)
- Long lifespan (50+ years)
- Environmental considerations (river flow)
- Complex permitting

**Best for:**
- Long-term investors
- Stable returns preference
- Impact-focused (often brings electricity to remote areas)

**Example projects:**
- Run-of-river micro-hydro (500 kW-5 MW, $200k-$2M)
- Small hydro with storage (10-50 MW, $5M-$25M)
- Pumped storage (100+ MW, institutional scale)

#### Energy Storage

**Characteristics:**
- Enabling technology for renewables
- Multiple revenue streams (arbitrage, grid services)
- Rapidly evolving technology
- Higher risk, higher return potential

**Best for:**
- Tech-savvy investors
- Higher risk tolerance
- Shorter timelines (5-7 years)

**Example projects:**
- Community battery (1-5 MWh, $100k-$500k)
- Grid-scale storage (10-100 MWh, $2M-$20M)
- Behind-the-meter commercial (500 kWh-2 MWh, $200k-$1M)

#### Nuclear (SMRs)

**Characteristics:**
- Baseload clean energy
- Long development timelines
- Regulatory complexity
- Large-scale impact potential

**Best for:**
- Very long-term investors (15+ years)
- Climate-focused investors
- Diversification from variable renewables

**Example projects:**
- Small Modular Reactor development ($50M+ rounds)
- Advanced reactor R&D ($10M-$50M rounds)
- Nuclear fusion research ($100M+ rounds)

### By Geography

#### Developed Markets

**Examples:** USA, UK, Germany, Australia, Canada

**Characteristics:**
- Lower political risk
- Strong regulatory frameworks
- Higher transparency
- Lower returns (7-10% typical)
- Strong rule of law

**Best for:** Conservative investors, first investments

#### Emerging Markets

**Examples:** India, Brazil, Vietnam, Kenya, Philippines

**Characteristics:**
- Higher growth potential
- Greater impact (energy access)
- Higher returns (10-15% typical)
- Higher political/currency risk
- Rapidly growing demand

**Best for:** Diversification, impact focus, higher risk tolerance

## Impact Stories

### Community Transformations

**Solwezi Solar - Zambia**

> Before: Village of 2,000 with no electricity, relying on diesel generators and kerosene lamps.
>
> After: 500 kW solar microgrid providing 24/7 power to homes, school, clinic, and businesses.
>
> **Impact:**
> - 450 households with electricity for first time
> - School can now use computers and internet
> - Clinic can refrigerate vaccines and operate at night
> - 15 new businesses opened (welding, refrigeration, phone charging)
> - 12 local jobs created
>
> **Investor Story:**
> "I invested $500 in 2023. I've already received $92 in returns, but seeing the photos of kids doing homework under electric lights makes it priceless." - *Sarah K., Terra Lumina investor*

**Appalachian Community Solar - West Virginia**

> Before: Former coal mining community facing economic depression, high energy costs.
>
> After: 2 MW community solar array providing discounted electricity to 300 low-income households.
>
> **Impact:**
> - Average $600/year savings per household
> - 45 construction jobs (many former miners)
> - 8 permanent operations jobs
> - Property tax revenue funding school improvements
> - Community ownership transition in 2033
>
> **Investor Story:**
> "As someone who grew up in coal country, investing in renewable energy that actually helps these communities transition feels like coming full circle." - *Mike R., Terra Lumina investor*
