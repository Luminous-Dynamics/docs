## The Traditional Exit Model

### How It Usually Works

**Year 0-10: The "Impact" Phase**

1. Fund raises capital from impact investors
2. Builds renewable energy project (solar, wind, hydro)
3. Project generates electricity and revenue
4. Investors receive returns (7-12% annually)
5. Everyone feels good about "making a difference"

**Year 10: The Exit**

6. Project sold to highest bidder
7. Highest bidder is **always** an extraction-focused entity
8. Why? They can pay more (they'll extract more)
9. Investors cash out
10. New owner maximizes profit

**Year 11+: The Extraction Phase**

11. Operating costs cut (lay off local workers)
12. Maintenance deferred (extract maximum near-term cash)
13. Rates raised (captive customers, maximize revenue)
14. Community benefit eliminated (cost center, removed)
15. Asset sweated until no longer profitable
16. Then sold again, or shut down

**Who wins?**
- Early investors: ‚úÖ Good returns
- Exit buyer: ‚úÖ Maximum extraction
- Fund managers: ‚úÖ Fees and carry

**Who loses?**
- Community: ‚ùå Lost local benefit
- Workers: ‚ùå Jobs eliminated
- Environment: ‚ùå Deferred maintenance, eventual closure
- Future generations: ‚ùå No lasting infrastructure

### The Fundamental Flaw

Impact investing says: **"We'll do good AND make returns!"**

But traditional exit model reveals the truth: **"We'll do good UNTIL we exit."**

The problem isn't the investors (who often genuinely want impact).

The problem isn't the operators (who often care about community).

**The problem is the exit.**

Once you sell to the highest bidder, **impact ends**. Because the highest bidder optimizes for extraction, not impact.

## How Regenerative Exit Actually Works

### The Structure

**Legal framework:**

```
Investment Structure:
‚îú‚îÄ Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) - Holds project
‚îú‚îÄ Investor Tokens - Represent ownership % and returns
‚îú‚îÄ Community Cooperative - Pre-formed, waiting
‚îî‚îÄ Transfer Agreement - Executed at year 10
```

**Investment phase (Year 0-10):**
- Investors hold tokens representing ownership %
- Tokens receive distributions from project revenue
- Tokens have **no resale market** (by design)
- 10-year term with clear exit date

**Transition phase (Year 9-10):**
- Community cooperative trained on operations
- Management knowledge transferred
- Legal ownership documents prepared
- Final investor distributions calculated

**Regenerative exit (Year 10):**
- Investor tokens redeemed at contracted value
- Ownership transfers to community cooperative
- Community receives all assets, free and clear
- Investors exit completely, returns fulfilled

**Regenerative phase (Year 11+):**
- Community owns and operates
- All revenue stays local
- Decisions made democratically
- Asset maintained for multi-generational benefit

### The Economics

**Example: $2M Solar Farm Project**

**Investment:**

**Year 10 Transfer:**
- Community receives:
  - 1 MW solar farm (market value: ~$1.5M)
  - All equipment and infrastructure
  - All permits and contracts
  - All operational knowledge
  - **Free and clear, no debt**

**Community ongoing benefit (Year 11+):**
- Annual revenue: $260K
- Operating costs: $80K
- **Annual community benefit: $180K**

## Common Questions

### "Why would investors accept no resale market?"

**Traditional thinking:** "I need liquidity. I need to sell if I want out."

**Regenerative reality:**
- 10-year term is clear upfront
- Returns are competitive with alternatives
- Early redemption options for hardship
- Many investors prefer defined timeline

**Plus:** No resale market means no speculation, no price volatility, no extraction pressure.

### "Why 10 years specifically?"

**Practical reasons:**
- Long enough to stabilize projects
- Long enough for good returns
- Short enough for human timescales
- Long enough to train community operators

**Financial reasons:**
- Most debt paid down
- Operations proven
- Revenue predictable
- Asset mature

**Community reasons:**
- Time to form cooperative
- Time to build trust
- Time to develop capabilities
- Time to plan for transition

### "What if the community can't operate it?"

**That's the entire point of 10 years.**

**Year 1-3: Partnership forming**
- Community cooperative established
- Legal structure created
- Initial governance formed

**Year 4-7: Training and shadowing**
- Community members trained
- Operations shadowed
- Decisions co-made
- Confidence built

**Year 8-10: Transition preparation**
- Community leading operations
- Original operators advising
- Knowledge transfer complete
- Ownership transfer finalized

**By year 10:** Community has been operating the asset for 2+ years already. The "transfer" is just legal‚Äîoperational transfer happened gradually.

### "What if investors want out early?"

**Hardship redemption:**
- Serious financial hardship
- Medical emergencies
- Other qualifying events
- Redeemed at fair value

**Transfer to others:**
- Can transfer to family, friends
- No price markup allowed
- Subject to KYC/AML
- Same 10-year term applies

**What you CAN'T do:**
- Speculate on resale
- Flip to highest bidder
- Extract early profits

**Why:** This is investment, not speculation. We want aligned long-term partners.

### "How do you prevent community corruption?"

**Governance structures:**
- Democratic decision-making
- Transparent financial reporting
- Third-party audits
- Public benefit corporation requirements

**Accountability:**
- Community members are also customers
- Local reputation matters
- Mismanagement affects them directly
- Natural incentive alignment

**Support:**
- Technical assistance available
- Peer network of other community-owned projects
- Industry association membership
- Best practices sharing

**Historical evidence:** Community-owned utilities have **better** track records than corporate-owned for long-term maintenance and community benefit. Their incentives align with outcomes.
